We have ONE rule:
Be excellent to each other!

Noisebridge is an anarchist hackerspace, where you can come and do everything you would like to - as long as you follow our one rule: Be excellent to others & yourself.
What does it mean to be excellent? Join us at our next weekly meeting to find out and discuss with others.

How to participate?

Most of what happens at Noisebridge is done via do-ocracy - if you want to see something happening, make it happen - as long as you are excellent to others and yourself. If you like to participate (which is super excellent!), you can do it in many ways, for example:

  • work on cool things you care about & have a great time at Noisebridge
  • donate money or hardware
  • get in conversations with others and ask what they work on & if you can help in any way
  • organize an event or support existing ones
  • help cleaning up the space
  • give welcoming tours to new people in the space
  • & much more.

Come to the next weekly General Meeting and join the conversation of how participation can look like at Noisebridge!

Safespace/Bravespace

All the excellent people coming to Noisebridge should be able to feel comfortable at the space. That means if anyone is harassing you / makes you feel unsafe, it’s perfectly excellent to ask that person to leave and take a break.

However, that doesn’t mean you aren’t able to discuss controversal topics at Noisebridge. But be mindful about having a rational, respectful, mature discussion with others, even if you strongly disagree with them.

If you feel a situation surpresses aspects of your speech and your speech wasn’t intended to be hurtful, the #bravespace channel on Slack and talking with people in the space in person are the right places to talk about it.

Noisebridge's history:
The Up's, Down's & Learnings

Like every community also Noisebridge had Up’s and Down’s -and we learned from it. All the way from our first down, followed by our Reboot, the most active time of Noisebridge ever in 2016-2017, followed by another down in 2018/early 2019 and since then a new up, with a lot of new people in the space and more and more people starting to get actively involved in making Noisebridge a wonderful place for all the excellent people coming here and rebuilding the cultural imune system.
